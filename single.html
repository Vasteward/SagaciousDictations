Holding Representatives Responsible & Accountable

Michael Cohen has been in the hot seat and under some very tenuous questioning from the entire congressional population in D.C. This is to corroborate his earlier statements, be in agreement with his testimony at his hearing, and clear his name of Trump’s misbehavior and law breaking.


Bottom Line

Voting matters.

Your vote matters. My vote matters. If the numbers of registered voters that voted had not voted, we would not be looking at this cancer that has overcome our political system. We would have had a cover-up of massive proportions and the criminal activity taking place at 1600 Pennsylvania Avenue would have gone unnoticed and unaccounted for. Do not forego your constitutional right to cast your vote for your chosen candidate.

It further takes us to task to do our part in making the decision based upon their campaign promises, their availability, and their dedication to their constituency, and most of all – refusing to use their office and their power to further their own personal agenda for profit and/or notoriety.


We must vow to hold our elected representatives responsible and accountable!!!

Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez

I am totally impressed with AOC. However, she is pushing the envelope at a rate that may result in burnout on her part.


I read today that she is paying her interns (known as newbies back in the day) at a rate of $52K per year. I am questioning this decision because it sends the wrong message and that message is that you don’t have to earn your pay, you don’t have to be an understudy in order to improve, you don’t have to have any seniority or experience under your belt – you are privileged to be in the employ of a person that has this plan to do IT differently. I think it’s a decision that will serve to bite her in the butt because I don’t care where you’re from or where you’re going. If you’ve been on a job or held a position for a while, you expect certain perks. It could be an annual raise, paid vacation, compensatory time, bonuses, et. al. It is not practicing good human resource standards to pay someone with little or no experience more than your experienced, senior personnel.


It is a well-known fact that airline pilots are routinely retired when they reach a certain age. However, Captain Sully was beyond retirement age when he so confidently set a plane down in the Hudson River and suffered no casualties. He could do that because he had recently completed a course on landing in water and he had enough experience under his belt to know what action to take to save the lives of his passengers. I will be willing to bet all I have that a novice pilot would not have had the acumen, nerve, or skill to complete the landing safely.


Now, before I go completely off my rocker, I will have to complete some in depth research on her, her background, her affiliations, and ultimately her vision for the people in her district.


I am addressing just one facet of this dilemma and I trust AOC will take this for the encouragement and advisement it was meant to be.


I believe she has fire in her belly and she is going to be a force to be reckoned with. BUT, she’s a kid in the big league. She should learn the rules before she begins to attempt to change them. There are people on Capitol Hill that are more than willing to mentor, guide, assist, share… She just needs to be open to their expertise and their cavernous wisdom.


-jw
